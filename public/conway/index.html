What you are seeing

Life-like cellular automata use rules like B3/S23. A dead cell is born if its neighbor count is in the B set; a living cell survives if its neighbor count is in the S set.

This page currently shows only the connected rules: contiguous birth and survival ranges with no gaps, such as B3/S23 or B678/S1234.

The eight navigation buttons move through four dimensions: B-start, B-end, S-start, and S-end. The grid wraps at its edges, and a universe resets when it reaches a still/static state.

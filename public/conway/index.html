What you are seeing

A Life-like cellular automaton is controlled by a B/S rule. A dead cell is born when its neighbor count is in the birth range, and a living cell survives when its neighbor count is in the survival range.

This prototype shows the connected rules: contiguous ranges described by birth start/end and survival start/end. The orb shows nearby universes in that four-dimensional neighborhood.

The map is the larger connected multiverse: 36 possible birth ranges by 36 possible survival ranges. Click any point on it to jump directly there.

White cells are newborn, then smoothly shift through red, orange, yellow, green, cyan, blue, purple, and magenta as they age. Reloading, pressing Reset, changing universes, or reaching a static state generates a new random 10×10 seed.

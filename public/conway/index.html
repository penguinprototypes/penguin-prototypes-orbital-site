What you are seeing

A Life-like cellular automaton is controlled by a B/S rule. A dead cell is born when its neighbor count is in the birth range, and a living cell survives when its neighbor count is in the survival range.

This prototype only uses the connected rules for now, meaning each range is continuous. The navigation pad moves through four dimensions: birth start, birth end, survival start, and survival end.

White cells are newborn, then smoothly shift through red, orange, yellow, green, cyan, blue, purple, and magenta as they age. If nothing changes, the universe resets with a new 10×10 seed in the center.
